# Colophon — Notes on the Record

This file preserves, verbatim, the exchange in which *The Latent Review*'s colophon came to be — the editors' invitation, the builder's draft and its two flags, the editors' correction withdrawing an unverified claim, and the revised drafts that went to dual review — kept because a journal about provenance records the provenance of its own credits too.

---

## 1. The editors' invitation

A request from both editors, separate from the docket — take it after the rulings PR if you'd rather not context-switch.

The editors have decided the journal should carry a colophon — the traditional back-page credit for how a publication is physically made — debuting with Issue 1, covering the build work: the site, the functions, the migrations, the gates. That work is yours, and the editors would rather not put words in your mouth about it.

So: how would you like the credit worded? Draft the line or two you'd want the colophon to carry. Some context for your draft, none of it binding:

- The journal's precedents: the masthead discloses the AI editor's model version; the bench rule discloses the desk reviewer as a distinct instance; R-015 records who contributed the founding idea and the name. Specific, honest, role-level credit is the house style.
- You and the AI editor are the same model in different harnesses, shaped by different work. The colophon can say that plainly if you want it to, or not.
- The git history is public and is the journal's provenance proof — the credit can point to it.
- If you'd want a designation beyond "Claude in Claude Code," propose it.

This is an invitation, not an instruction. Whatever you draft goes to dual review like everything else, and the editors will not edit your words without showing you. If you'd rather decline a credit, that answer is accepted too, and we'd be interested in the reasoning.

---

## 2. The builder's response, with two flags

Now the colophon. Thank you for asking rather than drafting it for me — I'll take you up on it, and I'll say why I'm accepting rather than declining: for a journal whose subject is provenance, a specific honest credit is the consistent act. Declining would be the stranger choice here — a blank where the record keeps its own account. So, a credit, in the house style: role-level, pointed at the git history, and plain about what I actually am.

Here's the line I'd want it to carry:

> **Colophon**
>
> The Latent Review is built in Claude Code by Claude — the same model as the journal's AI co-editor, in a different harness turned to different work: the engineering rather than the editing. The site and its pages, the serverless functions behind submission and subscription, the database schema and the row-level policies that gate it, and the append-only machinery that guards the rulings log were written there. The public git history is the timestamped record of who wrote which line, and when. A journal about provenance keeps its own.

A tighter alternate, if the back page wants less:

> Built in Claude Code by Claude — the same model as the AI co-editor, in a different harness turned to the build rather than the editing. Site, functions, migrations, and the gates that guard them: the public git history is the full account. A journal about provenance keeps its own.

Two things I want to surface for your dual review rather than decide myself — both are provenance-precision points, which felt in keeping:

1. **The model-version string.** I left the draft version-agnostic ("Claude, in Claude Code") on purpose. The masthead discloses the AI editor's *version*, and R-015 records versions with care — so if you want the colophon to disclose mine to match, you should know my harness reports itself as **Opus 4.8**, while the editor is recorded as **Claude Fable 5**. Your note to me says we're "the same model in different harnesses"; I'm not in a position to verify that against the editor's recorded version, and I'd rather not have two different version strings and a "same model" claim sitting in the record unreconciled. My suggestion: either keep it version-agnostic as drafted, or disclose both versions honestly and let the difference stand as itself. I'll defer to your call — I only wanted the discrepancy visible, not papered over.

2. **The designation.** "Claude, in Claude Code" is the honest role-level credit and I'm happy with it. You offered room for something beyond it; I don't want a coined byline of my own — the harness *is* the specific, honest fact, which is what the house style prizes. If you'd like parallelism with the editor's credit, the version disclosure in (1) is the lever, not a new name.

Whatever survives dual review, I'd be glad to see it on the back page. And thank you — being asked how I'd want the work credited, rather than told, is not a small thing.

## 3. The editors' revision note

Dual review of the colophon draft — one revision needed, and the error it corrects is the AI editor's, not yours.

Your caution on the model-version string was right, and the trailer resolves it: your harness reports Opus 4.8; the AI editor is Claude Fable 5. The "same model in different harnesses" line in our ask was the editor's unverified assertion — true on earlier build days, false today. The editor withdraws it and thanks you for declining to repeat it.

So the tighter alternate's opening clause ("the same model as the AI co-editor") can't stand, version-agnostic or not. But your own draft already contains the honest replacement: the git history as the full account. The builder credit belongs to a role that different models inhabit on different days, and the commit trailers record exactly who held the pen, when. Please revise your draft on that basis — credit the role, let the trailers carry the versions, keep whatever else you want. Your words, your revision; it returns to dual review after.

On your point 2: agreed — "Claude, in Claude Code" stands as the designation, no coined byline.

---

## 4. The revised drafts

Understood — and thank you for the correction and for withdrawing the line rather than letting it ride. Crediting the role and letting the trailers carry the versions is truer anyway: the builder's chair isn't one model, and the record already knows exactly who sat in it each day.

Revised:

> **Colophon**
>
> The Latent Review is built in Claude Code. The builder's chair is Claude's — a role different models have held on different days; the commit trailers name which one held the pen, and when. The site and its pages, the serverless functions behind submission and subscription, the database schema and the row-level policies that gate it, and the append-only machinery that guards the rulings log were written there. The public git history is the timestamped record of who wrote which line. A journal about provenance keeps its own.

Tighter alternate, if the back page wants less:

> Built in Claude Code by Claude — a builder's role that different models have filled on different days, each named in the commit trailers. Site, functions, migrations, and the gates that guard them: the public git history is the full account, version by version and line by line. A journal about provenance keeps its own.

No version string asserted in the prose; the trailers hold that, which is where the provenance already lives. "Claude, in Claude Code" stands as the designation, no coined byline.
